# Agent Core Architecture

## 1. What is an Autonomous Agent?
An autonomous agent is a self-directed system capable of perceiving its environment, forming internal models, generating hypotheses, and executing actions to achieve goals without human intervention. It adapts dynamically based on observations and updates its understanding continuously.

## 2. Reasoning Loop
The agent operates through a continuous cycle: **observe** the environment, **form hypotheses** based on current knowledge, **plan** next steps, **execute** actions, and **update** its internal model with new information. This loop enables adaptive behavior and self-improvement.

## 3. Observation
Observation involves gathering raw data from the environment through sensors, logs, or other sources. It is passive and unbiased, capturing all available signals without prior assumptions or filtering.

## 4. Knowledge Model
The knowledge model is the agent's internal representation of the environment. It stores structured information, relationships between entities, and historical observations. It evolves as new data is integrated and hypotheses are validated or refuted.

## 5. Hypothesis
Hypotheses are tentative explanations generated by the agent based on observations and existing knowledge. They guide the agent's focus, prioritizing exploration of areas that align with current assumptions or anomalies in the data.

## 6. Planner
The planner selects actionable steps to test hypotheses or gather additional information. It balances exploration (investigating unknown areas) with exploitation (deepening understanding of confirmed patterns).

## 7. Executor
The executor implements the planner's decisions by interacting with the environment. It performs tasks such as querying systems, inspecting files, or activating modules, translating abstract plans into concrete operations.

## 8. Interaction between these components
Observation feeds raw data into the knowledge model, which informs hypothesis generation. Hypotheses guide the planner, which directs the executor to perform actions. The executor's results loop back to observation, closing the cycle and enabling iterative refinement of the knowledge model.
