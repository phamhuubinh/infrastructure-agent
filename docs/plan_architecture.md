# Plan Architecture

## 1. Definition of a Plan  
A **Plan** is a structured sequence of **Actions** designed to achieve a specific **Goal** while adhering to **Constraints**. It represents a logical pathway from the current state of the environment to a desired end state. A Plan is composed of **Steps**, each of which is an **Action** with defined **Preconditions** and **Postconditions**.

---

## 2. Internal Plan Structure  
A Plan contains the following components:  
- **Goal**: The high-level objective the Plan aims to achieve.  
- **Steps**: A list of **Steps** (actions) required to achieve the Goal.  
- **Constraints**: Rules or limitations that the Plan must follow.  
- **Priority**: A value indicating the Plan's urgency or importance.  
- **Status**: The current state of the Plan (e.g., "draft", "validated", "executing", "completed", "failed").  
- **Metadata**: Additional information such as `created_at`, `updated_at`, and `planner_id`.  

---

## 3. Required Plan Fields  
Every Plan must include the following fields:  
- `goal` (string): The high-level objective.  
- `steps` (array of Step objects): The sequence of actions to achieve the goal.  
- `constraints` (array of strings or objects): Rules or limitations.  
- `priority` (integer or string): Determines execution order.  
- `status` (string): Current lifecycle state.  
- `created_at` (timestamp): When the Plan was created.  
- `updated_at` (timestamp): When the Plan was last modified.  

---

## 4. Internal Step Structure  
Each **Step** is an atomic unit within a Plan and includes:  
- `action` (string): The specific task to execute.  
- `preconditions` (array of strings or objects): Conditions that must be true before execution.  
- `postconditions` (array of strings or objects): Expected outcomes after execution.  
- `dependencies` (array of Step IDs): Steps that must complete before this one can execute.  
- `status` (string): Current state of the Step (e.g., "pending", "in_progress", "completed", "failed").  
- `result` (optional): Output or data generated by the Step.  

---

## 5. Required Step Fields  
Every Step must include the following fields:  
- `action` (string): The specific task to execute.  
- `preconditions` (array of strings or objects): Conditions that must be true before execution.  
- `postconditions` (array of strings or objects): Expected outcomes after execution.  
- `dependencies` (array of Step IDs): Steps that must complete before this one can execute.  
- `status` (string): Current state of the Step.  

---

## 6. Goal  
A **Goal** is a high-level objective derived from the **Knowledge Model** (e.g., anomalies, unexplored areas, or hypotheses). It defines what the Plan aims to achieve.

---

## 7. Action  
An **Action** is a specific, executable task or operation that modifies the environment. It is abstract and must be translated by the **Executor** into concrete operations (e.g., API calls, file reads).

---

## 8. Step  
A **Step** is a single **Action** within a **Plan**. Steps are ordered and may depend on the success or outcome of previous steps.

---

## 9. Constraint  
A **Constraint** is a rule or limitation that the **Plan** must follow (e.g., resource availability, safety requirements, or environmental conditions). Constraints ensure the **Plan** is feasible and aligns with the agent's capabilities.

---

## 10. Preconditions  
A **Precondition** is a condition that must be true **before** an **Action** can be executed. Preconditions ensure that an **Action** is applicable in the current state of the environment.

---

## 11. Postconditions  
A **Postcondition** is a condition that is guaranteed to be true **after** an **Action** is executed. Postconditions define the expected outcome or state change caused by the **Action**.

---

## 12. Expected Result  
The **Expected Result** is the anticipated output or state change resulting from the execution of an **Action**. It is defined by the **Postconditions** of the **Step**.

---

## 13. Retry Policy  
A **Retry Policy** defines how many times an **Action** should be retried if it fails, and under what conditions (e.g., exponential backoff, fixed delay).

---

## 14. Timeout  
A **Timeout** specifies the maximum time allowed for an **Action** to complete before it is considered failed.

---

## 15. Execution Mode  
**Execution Mode** defines whether **Steps** are processed **Sequentially** (one after another) or **Parallel** (simultaneously). This determines the order and dependencies of **Steps**.

---

## 16. Plan ID  
A **Plan ID** is a unique identifier assigned to a **Plan** for tracking and reference purposes.

---

## 17. Step ID  
A **Step ID** is a unique identifier assigned to a **Step** for tracking and reference purposes.

---

## 18. Plan Version  
A **Plan Version** indicates the iteration or revision number of a **Plan**, useful for tracking changes and updates.

---

## 19. Plan States  
A Plan transitions through the following states during its lifecycle:  
- **Draft**: Created but not yet validated.  
- **Validated**: Checked for feasibility and constraints.  
- **Executing**: Steps are being processed by the Executor.  
- **Completed**: All Steps have succeeded.  
- **Failed**: One or more Steps failed, or the Plan was aborted.  

---

## 20. Step States  
Each Step transitions through these states:  
- **Pending**: Ready to execute but not yet started.  
- **In Progress**: Currently being executed.  
- **Completed**: Successfully executed.  
- **Failed**: Execution failed or was interrupted.  

---

## 21. Plan Execution Lifecycle  
The lifecycle of a Plan includes the following stages:  
1. **Creation**: The Planner generates a Plan based on a Goal, Candidate Actions, and Constraints.  
2. **Validation**: The Plan is checked against the Knowledge Model to ensure feasibility.  
3. **Execution**: The Executor processes each Step in order.  
4. **Monitoring**: The Executor tracks the status of each Step.  
5. **Completion or Failure**: The Plan is marked as completed or failed.  
6. **Feedback Loop**: Results are fed back into the Knowledge Model for future planning.  

---

## 22. Information Flow  
- **Planner → Plan**: Generates a Plan with Goals, Steps, Constraints, and Priority.  
- **Validator → Plan**: Validates the Plan against the Knowledge Model.  
- **Executor → Step**: Executes Steps and updates their status.  
- **Executor → Knowledge Model**: Feeds back results from executed Steps.  
- **Knowledge Model → Planner**: Provides updated context for replanning.  

---

## 23. Failure Handling  
- If a **Step** fails, the **Executor** records the failure and updates the **Plan** status.  
- The **Planner** may trigger **Replanning** based on the failure and new information.  

---

## 24. Replanning  
**Replanning** occurs when new information (e.g., from the Executor or Knowledge Model) invalidates the current Plan. The Planner updates the Goal Queue, regenerates candidate actions, and re-evaluates prioritization to produce a revised Plan.

---

## 25. Relationship with Planner, Executor, and Knowledge Model  
- **Planner**: Generates Plans, validates them, and updates the Goal Queue.  
- **Executor**: Executes Steps, translates abstract Plans into concrete operations, and provides feedback.  
- **Knowledge Model**: Provides context for Goals, Constraints, and validates Preconditions and Postconditions.
